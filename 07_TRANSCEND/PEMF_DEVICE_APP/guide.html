What is PEMF?

PEMF = Pulsed ElectroMagnetic Field

PEMF therapy uses electromagnetic pulses to stimulate cellular activity in the body. Unlike static magnets, PEMF devices generate pulsing magnetic fields that can penetrate deep into tissues.

  1. Signal Generation: The controller generates an electrical signal at a specific frequency
  2. Coil Activation: Current flows through a coil, creating a magnetic field
  3. Pulsing: The field pulses on/off at the set frequency (e.g., 23 times per second at 23 Hz)
  4. Cellular Effect: Cells respond to the pulsing field

Frequency Controls

This app controls the pulse rate of your PEMF device - how many times per second the magnetic field turns on/off.

Available Frequencies

1-10 Hz Low frequency range
10-30 Hz Medium frequency range
23 Hz Custom preset frequency
30-100 Hz Higher frequency range

Intensity

Controls the strength of the magnetic field. Start low and increase gradually.

  • Low (10-30%): Gentle, for sensitive areas
  • Medium (30-60%): Standard use
  • High (60-100%): Maximum output

Waveform Types

The waveform determines how the magnetic field pulses - its shape over time.

Sine Wave

Smooth, natural oscillation

  • Gradual transitions
  • Most gentle output
  • Single frequency, no harmonics

Square Wave

Sharp on/off pulses

  • Instant transitions
  • Contains frequency harmonics
  • More stimulating output

Sawtooth Wave

Gradual rise, sharp drop

  • Rich in harmonics
  • Asymmetric pulse pattern
  • Deep tissue penetration

Pulse Wave

Short bursts with gaps

  • Brief, powerful pulses
  • Adjustable duty cycle
  • Most intense stimulation

Method 1: Simple Audio-Based PEMF (Easiest)

Parts Needed:

  • Old headphones or speaker (for the coil)
  • Audio amplifier (small class D amp, ~$10)
  • Phone or computer (to generate frequencies)
  • Magnet wire OR use existing speaker coil

Steps:

  1. Get/Make a Coil:

    Option A: Take apart old headphones - the speaker inside has a coil

    Option B: Wind 100-200 turns of magnet wire (22-26 AWG) around a 3-4" diameter form

  2. Connect to Amplifier:

    Connect coil to the speaker output of a small audio amplifier

  3. Generate Signal:

    Use this app or a tone generator app to create frequencies (1-100 Hz)

    Connect phone audio out to amplifier input

  4. Test:

    Start with low volume (intensity). You can test the field with a compass - it should wiggle at the frequency rate.

Method 2: Arduino-Based PEMF (More Control)

Parts Needed:

  • Arduino Nano/Uno (~$5-15)
  • MOSFET (IRF520 or similar)
  • Flyback diode (1N4007)
  • Resistor 10kΩ
  • PEMF coil (see below)
  • 12-24V power supply
  • Potentiometer for intensity (optional)
  • LCD display (optional)

ARDUINO CODE (simplified):

int pemfPin = 9;
float frequency = 23.0;  // Hz
int intensity = 128;     // 0-255 (PWM)

void setup() {
  pinMode(pemfPin, OUTPUT);
}

void loop() {
  // Calculate period
  float period = 1000.0 / frequency;
  float halfPeriod = period / 2;

  // Generate square wave
  analogWrite(pemfPin, intensity);
  delay(halfPeriod);
  analogWrite(pemfPin, 0);
  delay(halfPeriod);
}

Specifications:
- Wire: 22-24 AWG magnet wire (enameled copper)
- Turns: 50-100 turns
- Diameter: 4-8 inches (10-20 cm)
- Wind flat like a pancake, not cylindrical

SIDE VIEW:
    ═══════════════════════════  ◄── Should be flat
    (all windings in same plane)

Coil Building Steps:

  1. Get a flat surface (cardboard, plastic sheet)
  2. Start at center, secure wire with tape
  3. Wind outward in a spiral, keeping turns close together
  4. Wind 50-100 turns depending on desired strength
  5. Secure outer end with tape
  6. Leave 12" wire leads at both ends for connection
  7. Optional: Pot in epoxy or cover with fabric for durability

Coil Specifications by Use:

Use Diameter Turns Wire
Localized (knee, elbow) 4-6" 50-75 22 AWG
Larger area (back, torso) 8-12" 75-100 20-22 AWG
Full body mat Multiple coils 50 each 18-20 AWG

Method 3: Bluetooth PEMF (Works with This App!)

Parts Needed:

  • ESP32 board (~$8)
  • MOSFET driver circuit (same as Arduino)
  • PEMF coil
  • 12V power supply
  • 3D printed or purchased enclosure

The ESP32 can receive Bluetooth commands from this app. You'll need to:

  1. Program ESP32 with BLE GATT service matching our UUIDs
  2. Parse frequency, intensity, waveform commands
  3. Generate PWM output to drive MOSFET
  4. Pair with this app using the Connect button

Testing Your Device

Safety Tests (Do ALL of these!):

  1. Visual Check: No loose wires, proper insulation
  2. Resistance Test: Coil should read 1-20 ohms (not open/short)
  3. No Shorts: No continuity between power and ground when off
  4. Heat Test: Run for 5 min, check for excessive heat
  5. Field Test: Use compass near coil - should deflect with pulses

Measuring Field Strength:

You can use a smartphone magnetometer app to measure approximate field strength. PEMF devices typically produce 0.1-100 microtesla (μT) at the coil surface.

Safety Guidelines

DO NOT USE IF:

  • You have a pacemaker or defibrillator
  • You have any electronic implant
  • You are pregnant (without doctor approval)
  • You have epilepsy
  • You have active bleeding

USE CAUTION IF:

  • You have metal implants (pins, plates)
  • You have a history of seizures
  • You are taking blood thinners
  • You have low blood pressure
  • You are feeling unwell

BEST PRACTICES:

  • Start with low intensity, short sessions
  • Drink water before and after sessions
  • Allow 15+ min between sessions
  • Don't exceed 60 min per session
  • Stop if you feel any discomfort
  • Consult healthcare provider for medical use
