## CRISIS CLASSIFICATION

### Level 1: Minor Issue
- Individual customer complaint
- Small technical glitch
- Misunderstanding on social media
- Single negative review

**Response:** Standard customer service
**Timeline:** Within 24 hours
**Escalation:** None required

### Level 2: Moderate Crisis
- Multiple related complaints
- Service disruption (2-24 hours)
- Negative press mention
- Social media backlash (limited)
- Employee issue (non-public)

**Response:** Coordinated team response
**Timeline:** Within 4 hours
**Escalation:** Founder aware

### Level 3: Serious Crisis
- Major service outage (24+ hours)
- Data breach or security incident
- Public employee misconduct
- Significant negative media coverage
- Legal threat

**Response:** Crisis team activation
**Timeline:** Within 1 hour
**Escalation:** Founder leading

### Level 4: Critical Crisis
- Existential threat to business
- Major legal/regulatory action
- Widespread data breach
- Founder misconduct allegation
- Viral negative coverage

**Response:** Full crisis mode
**Timeline:** Immediate
**Escalation:** All hands, external support

## CRISIS SCENARIOS & RESPONSES

### Scenario 1: Service Outage

**Situation:** Platform is down, customers can't access

**Immediate Actions (First 30 minutes):**
1. Technical team investigates
2. Status page updated
3. Social media acknowledgment
4. Email to active users (if extended)

**Communication Template (Social/Status Page):**
```
We're aware that Character Forge is currently experiencing issues. Our team is investigating and working to restore service as quickly as possible.

We'll update here as we have more information.

We apologize for the inconvenience.
```

**Communication Template (Email - Extended Outage):**
```
Subject: Service Update: Character Forge Temporarily Unavailable

[NAME],

You may have noticed that Character Forge is currently unavailable.

Here's what we know:
- [Brief description of issue]
- We're working to restore service
- Estimated resolution: [Time if known]

What this means for you:
- [Impact on their access/data]
- Your data is safe
- [Any workarounds]

We'll email again when service is restored.

We apologize for the disruption.

[NAME]
Character Forge Team
```

**Resolution Communication:**
```
Subject: Service Restored

[NAME],

Character Forge is back online.

What happened:
- [Brief, honest explanation]

What we're doing:
- [Prevention measures]

Your data was not affected.

Thank you for your patience.

[NAME]
```

### Scenario 2: Data/Security Breach

**Situation:** Customer data potentially compromised

**Immediate Actions (First Hour):**
1. Contain the breach (technical)
2. Assess scope
3. Legal notification (determine requirements)
4. Document everything
5. Prepare communication

**Do NOT:**
- Speculate publicly
- Delete evidence
- Wait to communicate once facts known

**Communication Template (Initial):**
```
Subject: Important Security Notice

[NAME],

We're writing to inform you of a security incident that may have affected your Character Forge account.

What happened:
[Brief factual description]

What information may be affected:
[Specific data types]

What we're doing:
- [Immediate actions taken]
- [Investigation steps]
- [Third-party assistance]

What you should do:
- [Specific actions for user]
- [Password change if needed]
- [Monitoring recommendations]

We take this seriously. We're committed to transparency and will update you as we learn more.

Questions? Contact [dedicated email/phone]

[NAME]
CEO, Character Forge
```

**Follow-up Communications:**
- Regular updates (even if "still investigating")
- Final report with root cause and prevention
- Offer of credit monitoring if personal data affected

### Scenario 3: Negative Media Coverage

**Situation:** Article or post portraying company negatively

**Assessment Questions:**
- Is it factually accurate?
- How widespread is the reach?
- What's the sentiment in comments?
- Is it gaining traction?

**Response Decision Tree:**

```
Is it accurate?
├── Yes → Acknowledge, explain context, share improvements
├── Partially → Clarify facts, acknowledge valid points
└── No → Correct factually, provide evidence

Is it gaining traction?
├── Yes → Proactive response
└── No → Monitor, prepare response if needed

Are comments hostile?
├── Yes → Respond once, don't engage in arguments
└── No → Engage genuinely with questions
```

**Response Principles:**
- Acknowledge quickly
- Don't be defensive
- Correct only clear factual errors
- Take valid criticism seriously
- Don't argue in public
- One clear response, then monitor

**Response Template:**
```
Thank you for raising this.

[If valid criticism]: We hear this feedback and take it seriously. [What we're doing about it]

[If factual error]: We want to clarify one point: [correct fact with evidence]

[If mixed]: Some of these points are fair and we're addressing them. We want to add context on [specific point].

We're committed to [relevant principle]. Happy to discuss further at [contact].
```

### Scenario 4: Customer Public Complaint

**Situation:** Customer posts negative experience publicly

**Assessment:**
- Is the complaint valid?
- Have we failed this customer?
- What's our history with them?
- Is this gaining attention?

**Response Approach:**

**If Valid Complaint:**
```
[NAME], thank you for sharing this. You're right that [acknowledge specific failure].

I'm sorry this was your experience.

I'd like to make this right. Can you DM me or email [address] so we can resolve this directly?

[Your name]
```

**If Misunderstanding:**
```
[NAME], I'm sorry you've had a frustrating experience.

I think there may be a misunderstanding about [specific point].

Would you be open to a quick conversation to sort this out? DM me or email [address].

[Your name]
```

**If Unreasonable/Bad Faith:**
```
[NAME], we're sorry Character Forge didn't meet your expectations.

We'd be happy to discuss your experience - please reach out to [email].

[No further public engagement if unreasonable]
```

**After Resolution:**
- Ask if they'll update their post
- Don't demand or pressure
- Thank them regardless

### Scenario 5: Employee/Leadership Issue

**Situation:** Misconduct allegation against team member

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Don't comment publicly until facts known
2. Document allegation
3. Internal investigation (or external if serious)
4. Legal consultation
5. Prepare holding statement

**Holding Statement:**
```
We're aware of the allegations regarding [person/situation].

We take these matters seriously.

We're gathering facts before making any public comment.

We will provide an update when we have more information.
```

**If Allegations Substantiated:**
```
Subject: [Situation] Update

Following a thorough [internal/external] review, we have determined that [brief factual finding].

We have taken the following actions:
- [Actions taken]
- [Personnel changes if applicable]

This does not reflect our values or the culture we're building.

We're committed to:
- [Specific preventive measures]
- [Training/policy changes]
- [Accountability measures]

We apologize to anyone affected.

[NAME]
CEO
```

### Scenario 6: Product/Methodology Criticism

**Situation:** Criticism of Pattern Theory or approach validity

**Assessment:**
- Is this legitimate academic/professional critique?
- Is this competitor attack?
- Is this customer misunderstanding?

**Response by Type:**

**Legitimate Critique:**
- Engage seriously and respectfully
- Acknowledge valid points
- Explain methodology
- Share evidence
- Thank them for the engagement

**Competitor Attack:**
- Don't engage in public fight
- One factual response if needed
- Focus on customers, not competitors

**Customer Misunderstanding:**
- Educate gently
- Provide resources
- Offer conversation

**Response Template (Methodology Question):**
```
Thanks for engaging with our approach critically.

Pattern Theory is based on [research basis] and draws from [fields]. We don't claim it's the only approach - it's one framework we've found effective for leadership transformation.

Our methodology:
- [Brief explanation]
- [Evidence/outcomes]
- [Limitations we acknowledge]

Happy to discuss further. We believe in transparent dialogue about what works and what we're still learning.
```

## COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES

### During Any Crisis:

1. **Speed matters, but accuracy matters more**
   - Acknowledge quickly
   - Promise update
   - Deliver accurate information

2. **Transparency builds trust**
   - Admit what you know
   - Admit what you don't know
   - Don't hide or minimize

3. **Empathy first**
   - Lead with understanding of impact
   - Apologize genuinely when appropriate
   - Don't be defensive

4. **One voice**
   - Designated spokesperson
   - Consistent messaging
   - Internal alignment before external

5. **Action over apology**
   - Words matter less than actions
   - Commit to specific changes
   - Follow through

### What NOT to Do:

- Don't speculate
- Don't blame customers or partners
- Don't delete/hide negative feedback
- Don't argue in public
- Don't go silent for too long
- Don't make promises you can't keep
- Don't be defensive
- Don't use manipulative tactics (even in crisis)

## POST-CRISIS REVIEW

### Debrief Template:

**Within 1 Week of Resolution:**

1. **What happened?** (Timeline and facts)
2. **What was the impact?** (Customers, reputation, operations)
3. **How did we respond?** (What worked, what didn't)
4. **What could we have done better?**
5. **What systems failed?**
6. **What preventive measures do we need?**
7. **What did we learn?**

### Implementation Follow-up:

- Assign prevention tasks
- Set deadlines
- Review in 30 days
- Update playbook with learnings
