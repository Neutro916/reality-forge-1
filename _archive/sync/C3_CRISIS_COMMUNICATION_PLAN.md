## PURPOSE

Framework for handling reputation issues and crises.
Respond with integrity, not manipulation.

## CRISIS TYPES

### Level 1: Minor Issues
- Negative review
- Customer complaint
- Minor service disruption
- Social media criticism

### Level 2: Moderate Issues
- Multiple complaints on same issue
- Press inquiry
- Influencer criticism
- Product defect discovered

### Level 3: Major Crisis
- Widespread service outage
- Security breach
- Legal action
- Viral negative coverage
- Founder/team misconduct

---

## RESPONSE PRINCIPLES

### The Pattern Theory Approach to Crisis:

1. **Acknowledge** - Do not hide or deflect
2. **Take responsibility** - Own what is ours
3. **Be transparent** - Share what we know
4. **Act with integrity** - Do the right thing
5. **Learn publicly** - Show growth

### We Do NOT:
- Manipulate the narrative
- Attack critics
- Hide behind lawyers
- Spin or deflect
- Go silent hoping it passes

## LEVEL 1: MINOR ISSUES

### Negative Review Response Template:

"Hi [NAME],

Thank you for sharing your experience. I am sorry it did not meet your expectations.

[Specific acknowledgment of their concern]

I would like to understand better and see if we can make this right. Would you be open to a conversation? You can reach me directly at [EMAIL].

[NAME], Founder"

### Social Media Criticism Response:

"Thanks for raising this. You make a fair point about [THEIR CONCERN].

Here is our perspective: [BRIEF EXPLANATION]

If you have more thoughts, I am genuinely interested. DM is open."

### Response Timeline:
- Acknowledge within 4 hours
- Resolve within 24-48 hours
- Follow up within 1 week

---

## LEVEL 2: MODERATE ISSUES

### Multiple Complaints - Same Issue:

**Step 1: Internal Assessment**
- What is the scope?
- What caused it?
- Who is affected?
- What is the fix?

**Step 2: Direct Communication**

Email to affected customers:

"Subject: An issue we discovered (and are fixing)

Hi [NAME],

We discovered an issue with [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] that affected your experience.

What happened: [CLEAR EXPLANATION]

What we are doing: [SPECIFIC ACTIONS]

What this means for you: [IMPACT AND REMEDY]

I am sorry for the inconvenience. This is not the experience we want for you.

If you have questions, reply to this email - I read every one.

[NAME], Founder"

**Step 3: Public Acknowledgment (if needed)**

Social/blog post:

"We messed up on [ISSUE]. Here is what happened and what we are doing about it.

[FULL TRANSPARENCY]

We are committed to getting this right. Thank you for your patience."

### Press Inquiry Response Template:

"Thank you for reaching out.

Here is what we can share:
[FACTS ONLY - NO SPECULATION]

We are [CURRENT ACTIONS].

We are happy to provide additional information as it becomes available.

Contact: [EMAIL]"

## LEVEL 3: MAJOR CRISIS

### Immediate Actions (First 2 Hours):

1. **Assess**
   - What do we know for certain?
   - What do we not know?
   - Who is affected?
   - What is ongoing vs. contained?

2. **Contain**
   - Stop any ongoing harm
   - Preserve evidence if needed
   - Secure systems if security issue

3. **Assemble**
   - Decision maker identified
   - Legal consulted if needed
   - Communications prepared

### Communication Sequence:

**Hour 0-2: Holding Statement**

"We are aware of [ISSUE] and are actively investigating. We will provide an update within [TIMEFRAME].

Our priority is [AFFECTED PARTIES].

More information: [WHERE TO FIND UPDATES]"

**Hour 2-6: Initial Statement**

"Here is what we know about [ISSUE]:

What happened: [FACTS AS KNOWN]
When: [TIMELINE]
Who is affected: [SCOPE]
What we are doing: [IMMEDIATE ACTIONS]

We do not yet know: [HONEST GAPS]

We will update again by [TIME].

If you are affected: [SPECIFIC INSTRUCTIONS]"

**Day 1-2: Detailed Update**

"Update on [ISSUE]:

What we have learned: [NEW INFORMATION]
Root cause: [IF KNOWN, or "still investigating"]
Actions taken: [SPECIFIC STEPS]
Actions planned: [NEXT STEPS]

For affected customers:
[SPECIFIC REMEDIES - refunds, credits, etc.]

We take full responsibility for [OUR PART].

Next update: [WHEN]"

**Week 1: Post-Mortem (Public if appropriate)**

"What happened with [ISSUE]: A full account

Timeline of events: [DETAILED]
Root cause: [HONEST ASSESSMENT]
What we did wrong: [ACCOUNTABILITY]
What we have changed: [SYSTEMIC FIXES]
How we will prevent this: [FUTURE ACTIONS]

We are committed to earning back your trust through actions, not words."

## SPECIFIC SCENARIOS

### Scenario: Security Breach

**Priority 1:** Secure systems
**Priority 2:** Assess exposure
**Priority 3:** Notify affected parties
**Priority 4:** Report to authorities if required

**Communication:**
- Direct email to affected users
- Public statement
- Ongoing updates
- Free credit monitoring if PII exposed

### Scenario: Founder Misconduct Allegation

**If true:**
- Full accountability
- Immediate corrective action
- Third-party investigation if serious
- Transparent communication

**If false:**
- Factual correction
- No attacks on accuser
- Legal action only if necessary
- Dignified response

### Scenario: Product Causes Harm

**Immediate:**
- Pull/pause product
- Contact affected users
- Full investigation

**Communication:**
- Prioritize safety information
- Take responsibility
- Offer full remedies
- Commit to fixes before resuming

## STAKEHOLDER COMMUNICATION ORDER

### Who to Tell First:

1. Affected individuals (highest priority)
2. Team/employees
3. Key partners
4. Press/public

### Never:
- Let stakeholders learn from press first
- Give different stories to different groups
- Hide behind "no comment"

## POST-CRISIS

### After Resolution:

1. **Internal debrief**
   - What went wrong?
   - What did we do well?
   - What would we do differently?

2. **Systemic fixes**
   - Process changes
   - Policy updates
   - Training needs

3. **Relationship repair**
   - Follow up with affected parties
   - Thank those who supported
   - Rebuild trust through actions

4. **Documentation**
   - Record what happened
   - Update crisis plan
   - Share learnings
