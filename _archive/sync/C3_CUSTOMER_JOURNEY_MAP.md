## STAGE 1: AWARENESS

### Customer State:
- Experiencing leadership challenges
- May not know "manipulation patterns" exist
- Frustrated with ineffective development
- Searching for solutions

### Entry Points:

| Channel | Trigger | Content |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Search | "why leadership training doesn't work" | Blog post |
| LinkedIn | Scroll, algorithm | Short-form content |
| Podcast | Interview | Guest appearance |
| Referral | Colleague recommendation | Direct link |
| Event | Conference/webinar | Speaking |

### Key Questions:
- "Why do I keep hitting the same walls?"
- "Why doesn't training stick?"
- "Am I the problem?"
- "Is there something different?"

### Emotional State:
Frustrated → Curious

### Touchpoints:
1. **Blog Content** - "5 Signs You're Manipulating Without Knowing It"
2. **Social Posts** - Pattern recognition examples
3. **Podcast Interviews** - Credibility and story
4. **Video Content** - YouTube explainers
5. **PR/Media** - Third-party credibility

### Success Metrics:
- Website traffic
- Social engagement
- Email list growth
- Brand search volume

### Opportunities:
- SEO optimization
- Content partnerships
- Podcast tour
- LinkedIn presence

### Friction Points:
- "Manipulation" word might trigger defensiveness
- Competing content noise
- Low awareness of category

### Actions to Take:
- Lead with curiosity, not accusation
- Use "patterns" more than "manipulation" early
- Provide immediate value in free content

## STAGE 2: CONSIDERATION

### Customer State:
- Aware manipulation patterns exist
- Curious about own patterns
- Evaluating options
- Comparing approaches

### Key Questions:
- "Do I have these patterns?"
- "Can this really be changed?"
- "How is this different from other training?"
- "What will it cost (time, money, effort)?"

### Emotional State:
Curious → Interested (but skeptical)

### Touchpoints:
1. **Assessment Landing Page** - Clear value proposition
2. **Case Studies** - Proof it works
3. **FAQ Content** - Objection handling
4. **Webinars** - Deeper education
5. **Email Nurture** - Building trust
6. **Comparison Content** - "Character Forge vs."

### Success Metrics:
- Landing page conversion
- Webinar registration
- Email engagement
- Time on site

### Opportunities:
- Free mini-assessment
- Value-first email sequence
- Testimonial videos
- ROI calculator

### Friction Points:
- Skepticism about "another training"
- Uncertainty about fit
- Time concern
- Price sensitivity

- Provide social proof heavily
- Offer risk-free entry points
- Address "why this is different" clearly
- Show specific outcomes

## KEY MOMENTS OF TRUTH

### Moment 1: First Pattern Recognition
When customer first recognizes their own pattern.
**Make it:** Validating, not shaming.

### Moment 2: First Application
When customer catches themselves in real-time.
**Make it:** Celebratory, encouraging.

### Moment 3: First External Feedback
When someone notices the change.
**Make it:** Documented, shared.

### Moment 4: Transformation Realization
When customer knows they've changed.
**Make it:** Milestone, celebrated.

### Moment 5: First Share
When customer tells someone else.
**Make it:** Easy, supported.

---

## JOURNEY GAPS TO ADDRESS

### Gap 1: Awareness → Consideration
Many aware but not converting to consideration.
**Solution:** Clearer differentiation, immediate value preview.

### Gap 2: Consideration → Decision
High consideration, lower conversion.
**Solution:** Risk reduction, payment flexibility, social proof.

### Gap 3: Onboarding → Value
Dropout during early engagement.
**Solution:** Faster time to value, clearer path, better support.

### Gap 4: Value → Expansion
Satisfied but not expanding.
**Solution:** Proactive offers, team invitation, clear next steps.

### Gap 5: Expansion → Advocacy
Using but not referring.
**Solution:** Easy referral mechanism, recognition, community.
