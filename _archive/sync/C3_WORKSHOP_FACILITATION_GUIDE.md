## PURPOSE

Guide for delivering live Pattern Theory workshops.
For internal use or licensed facilitators.

## WORKSHOP OVERVIEW

**Name:** Discover Your Blind Spots
**Duration:** 2.5-3 hours
**Participants:** 10-30 optimal
**Format:** In-person or virtual

---

## LEARNING OBJECTIVES

By end of workshop, participants will:
1. Understand the difference between influence and manipulation
2. Complete the Seven Domains assessment
3. Identify their primary blind spot
4. Develop a personal action plan
5. Have peer accountability partner

## DETAILED AGENDA

### OPENING (15 min)

**0:00-0:05 - Welcome**
- Facilitator introduction
- Logistics (breaks, bathrooms, phones)
- Confidentiality agreement

**0:05-0:15 - Icebreaker**
"Share one word that describes your leadership style - and one word your team might use."

Note: Often these are different. That gap is what we are exploring.

---

### MODULE 1: THE PROBLEM (20 min)

**0:15-0:25 - The Trust Gap**

Content:
- 68% stat on trust
- $23B turnover cost
- The manipulation epidemic concept

Facilitation:
"By show of hands, how many have worked for a leader they did not trust?"
[Wait for response]
"How many have wondered if your team fully trusts you?"
[Pause for reflection]

**0:25-0:35 - Influence vs Manipulation**

Content:
- Definitions and distinctions
- The intent question
- Why strategic feels manipulative

Activity:
"Think of a time you used influence successfully. What made it influence rather than manipulation?"
[2 min individual reflection, 3 min pair share]

---

### MODULE 2: THE FRAMEWORK (30 min)

**0:35-0:50 - Seven Domains Introduction**

Walk through each domain with examples:
1. Physical - body language, space, presence
2. Financial - rewards, resources, compensation
3. Mental - information, framing, timing
4. Emotional - feelings, vulnerability, guilt
5. Social - alliances, pressure, exclusion
6. Creative - ideas, credit, innovation
7. Integration - personas, authenticity, consistency

For each domain, ask:
"Can anyone share an example you have seen - not necessarily your own?"

**0:50-1:05 - Domain Deep Dive**

Choose 2-3 domains for deeper exploration based on audience.

Activity for each:
"When does [domain] cross from influence to manipulation?"
[Small group discussion, 5 min]
[Report out key insights]

---

### BREAK (10 min)

**1:05-1:15 - Break**

Remind participants to complete assessment if they have not.

---

### MODULE 3: THE ASSESSMENT (35 min)

**1:15-1:30 - Assessment Completion**

If not pre-completed:
- Guide to assessment link
- 15 minutes to complete
- Quiet reflection time

If pre-completed:
- Review results individually
- Note highest domain
- Note surprises

**1:30-1:50 - Results Interpretation**

Content:
- Score ranges and what they mean
- Domain breakdown interpretation
- Common patterns

Activity:
"Find someone whose highest domain is different from yours. Share what you learned about yourself."
[10 min pair discussion]

Debrief:
- What patterns emerged?
- Any surprises?
- What resonated most?

---

### MODULE 4: THE TRANSFORMATION (30 min)

**1:50-2:05 - Stories of Change**

Share 2-3 transformation stories:
- Starting point (pattern)
- Trigger (recognition)
- Process (change)
- Outcome (results)

Discussion:
"What gave these leaders the courage to change?"

**2:05-2:20 - Action Planning**

Individual work on action plan template:

1. My primary blind spot domain: ____________
2. One specific pattern I recognized: ____________
3. The cost of this pattern (to me, to others): ____________
4. One thing I will do differently this week: ____________
5. How I will know if it is working: ____________

**2:20-2:30 - Accountability Partnerships**

Activity:
"Find a partner. Share your action plan. Exchange contact info. Commit to checking in within 2 weeks."

---

### CLOSING (15 min)

**2:30-2:40 - Integration**

Go around the room:
"One word or phrase you are taking from today."

**2:40-2:45 - Resources and Next Steps**

- Remind about AI Coach access
- Share course information
- Provide contact for questions

**2:45-2:50 - Evaluation and Close**

- Feedback form (digital or paper)
- Thank participants
- Final thought: "The patterns you cannot see are the ones running your life. Now you can see. What you do next is up to you."

## FACILITATION TIPS

### Do:
- Create psychological safety first
- Use personal examples (vulnerability)
- Allow silence after questions
- Validate all responses
- Keep energy up after break
- Watch time closely

### Do Not:
- Call people out publicly
- Share specific participant results
- Rush the assessment
- Skip the action planning
- Lecture without interaction
- Judge any patterns revealed

---

## HANDLING DIFFICULT MOMENTS

### Resistant Participant:
"I appreciate that perspective. Others may feel differently. What would it take to test the framework?"

### Emotional Response:
"Thank you for sharing that. This work can bring up a lot. Would you like to take a moment?"

### Dominant Talker:
"Great insight. Let us hear from someone who has not shared yet."

### Off-Topic:
"Interesting point. Let us capture that for later. Right now we are focused on [topic]."

### Technical Failure:
Have paper assessments as backup. Have printed slides if projector fails.
