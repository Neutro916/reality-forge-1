## NONPROFIT-SPECIFIC MANIPULATION PATTERNS

### Pattern 1: Mission Martyrdom
"We can't afford to pay market rate—we're a nonprofit."
Using mission to justify under-compensation and overwork.

**Recognition:** Mission as excuse for exploitation.
**Transformation:** Sustainable compensation enables sustainable mission.

### Pattern 2: Donor Deference
"The donor wants it this way."
Using donor preferences to avoid accountability.

**Recognition:** Donor influence beyond appropriate scope.
**Transformation:** Donor partnership with mission integrity.

### Pattern 3: Urgency of Need
"People are suffering while we discuss this."
Using beneficiary needs to rush decisions.

**Recognition:** Real needs weaponized to prevent deliberation.
**Transformation:** Thoughtful action serves better than reactive scrambling.

### Pattern 4: Passion Exploitation
"You should feel lucky to do meaningful work."
Using purpose alignment to extract unpaid labor.

**Recognition:** Meaningful work used to justify poor conditions.
**Transformation:** Meaning AND sustainability. Both required.

### Pattern 5: Scarcity Mentality
"We don't have resources for that."
Using budget constraints to avoid difficult changes.

**Recognition:** Scarcity as permanent excuse, not temporary reality.
**Transformation:** Creative resourcefulness within honest constraints.

### Pattern 6: Impact Inflation
"We're changing lives" (without measurement).
Using emotional impact claims to avoid accountability.

**Recognition:** Story over data. Feeling over measurement.
**Transformation:** Rigorous impact measurement alongside stories.
