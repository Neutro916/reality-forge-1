## PRIMARY PERSONA: THE AWAKENING EXECUTIVE

### Demographics
- **Name:** Sarah
- **Age:** 38-52
- **Title:** VP, Director, or C-Suite
- **Company Size:** 100-5,000 employees
- **Industry:** Tech, Finance, Healthcare, Professional Services
- **Income:** $150K-$400K
- **Location:** Urban/Suburban, US primarily

### Psychographics
- **Values:** Results, efficiency, growth, recognition
- **Fears:** Being seen as weak, losing control, career stagnation
- **Aspirations:** Legacy, impact, authentic success
- **Self-Image:** Strategic, driven, effective

### The Trigger Moment
Something happened that cracked the facade:
- Key employee quit citing trust issues
- 360 feedback revealed blind spots
- Personal relationship suffered from work patterns
- Moment of self-recognition during conflict

### Current State
- Successful by external measures
- Nagging sense something is off
- Team performs but does not thrive
- Exhausted from maintaining image
- Curious but skeptical of soft solutions

### Pain Points
- "Why do not people trust me when I am trying to help them?"
- "I know I am strategic, but is that wrong?"
- "My team performs but there is no loyalty"
- "I feel like I am always managing perceptions"

### Goals
- Build genuine influence without manipulation
- Create a team that wants to follow
- Lead authentically without losing effectiveness
- Leave a positive legacy

### Objections
- "I do not have time for soft skills training"
- "This sounds like therapy, not leadership"
- "I am already successful, why change?"
- "Will this make me less effective?"

### Message That Resonates
"You have achieved a lot by being strategic. But there is a cost you are starting to see. What if you could be even more effective by being real?"

### Channels
- LinkedIn (primary)
- Executive podcasts
- Harvard Business Review
- Industry conferences
- Executive coaching referrals

## ANTI-PERSONA: WHO WE DO NOT WANT

### The Tactic Collector
- Wants manipulation techniques, not transformation
- Looking for shortcuts to influence
- No interest in self-reflection
- Will be disappointed and leave bad reviews

### The Skeptical Cynic
- Believes all leadership is manipulation
- Not open to alternative perspectives
- Will argue in community and drain energy
- Unlikely to complete program

### The Competitor
- Researching to copy methodology
- No intention of genuine engagement
- May request refund after extracting content
- Could create legal/IP issues

### The Freeloader
- Only wants free content
- Will not convert to paid
- Drains support resources
- Low engagement, high complaints

## PERSONA JOURNEY MAPS

### Awakening Executive Journey

1. **Awareness:** Trigger moment creates opening
2. **Research:** Searches leadership, trust, influence
3. **Discovery:** Finds assessment or content
4. **Evaluation:** Takes assessment, reviews results
5. **Decision:** Weighs investment vs. status quo
6. **Purchase:** Buys program
7. **Experience:** Goes through transformation
8. **Advocacy:** Shares with peers, refers team

### Rising Leader Journey

1. **Awareness:** Promoted or sees bad example
2. **Research:** Seeks leadership development
3. **Discovery:** Finds content via LinkedIn/podcast
4. **Evaluation:** Consumes free content, builds trust
5. **Decision:** Budget-conscious, needs clear value
6. **Purchase:** Starts with lower-cost option
7. **Experience:** Applies immediately
8. **Advocacy:** Vocal on social media

### HR Champion Journey

1. **Awareness:** Engagement/retention problem
2. **Research:** Searches for solutions
3. **Discovery:** Finds via conference, referral, or search
4. **Evaluation:** Requests info, compares vendors
5. **Decision:** Needs stakeholder buy-in
6. **Purchase:** Pilot program or team license
7. **Experience:** Measures outcomes
8. **Advocacy:** Case study, conference speaker
