## FINANCE-SPECIFIC MANIPULATION PATTERNS

### Pattern 1: Numbers Weaponization
Using data selectively to support predetermined conclusions.
"The numbers speak for themselves" (after cherry-picking).

**Recognition:** Data presented without context, opposing data ignored.
**Transformation:** Complete picture. Honest analysis. Acknowledge uncertainty.

### Pattern 2: Risk-Fear Manipulation
"If we don't do this, we'll lose everything."
Using risk language to shut down alternatives.

**Recognition:** Catastrophizing to prevent discussion.
**Transformation:** Balanced risk assessment. Multiple scenarios. Real probabilities.

### Pattern 3: Expertise Intimidation
"You wouldn't understand the financial complexities."
Using technical knowledge to silence non-experts.

**Recognition:** Complexity as barrier rather than bridge.
**Transformation:** Explain until understood. Expertise as service.

### Pattern 4: Compensation Weaponization
"Your bonus depends on this."
Using financial incentives as control mechanisms.

**Recognition:** Money used to bypass ethics and judgment.
**Transformation:** Aligned incentives. Values-based compensation.

### Pattern 5: Client-Blaming
"They should have read the fine print."
Shifting responsibility to avoid accountability.

**Recognition:** Technical compliance masking ethical failure.
**Transformation:** Fiduciary mindset. Client interests first.

### Pattern 6: Regulatory Shield
"We can't discuss that—compliance."
Using regulations to avoid difficult conversations.

**Recognition:** Compliance as excuse rather than genuine constraint.
**Transformation:** Spirit of regulation, not just letter.
